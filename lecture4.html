The Girvan-Newman Methodg

  • Repeat following steps as long as edges remain in graph
    1. find the edge of highest betweenness and remove from graph
    2. recalculate all betweennesses

Computing Betweeness

  • Algorithm
    1. Perform a breadth-first search of the graph, starting at A
    2. Determine the number of shortest paths from A to each other node
    3. Based on these numbers, determine the amount of flow from A to all other nodes that uses each edge

Overlapping Communities

  • Is it realistic/useful to assign nodes to only a single community?
  • Communities overlap pervasively, making it impossible to partition the networks without splitting communities

Overlapping Communities - Algorithms

  • CFinder
    • algorithm based on the clique percolation method
      • Identify k-cliques: a fully connected subgraph k nodes
      • Pair of k-cliques are "adjacent" if they share k−1 nodes
      • Form overlapping communities from maximal union of k-cliques that can be reached from each other through adjacent k-cliques
  • Greedy Clique Expansion (GCE)
    • identify distinct cliques as seeds, expands the seeds by greedily optimising a local fitness function
  • MOSES
    • scalable approach for identifying highlyoverlapping communities
      • Randomly select an edge, greedily expand a community around the edge to optimise an objective function
      • Delete "poor quality" communities
      • Fine-tune communities by re-assigning individual nodes

Attribute Networks

  • Based on the idea of homophily
  • Example - build a “suggest a friend” mechanism
    • treating an attribute or interest matrix as a 2-mode network
    • treat every one of the pieces of information (tags, keywords, etc.) as a node in a 2-mode network
    • compute a person-to-person affiliation network
    • apply the island method or clustering to find potential groupings of people
    • to suggest friends, simply pick the top links in the affiliation network

  • Inverse affiliation network - attributes through people
  • Example - mapping political discourse on Twitter.
    • harvest tweets from people with #election
    • build a 2-mode network from people to hashtags
    • compute a hashtag-through-people affiliation network
    • clusters will act as prox- ies for entire areas of discourse—and will likely separate liberal and conservative

Multimode Networks

  • PP * PP = PP
    • The new network means “who are friends-of-friends?”
  • PT * PTT = PT * TP = PP
    • this means: “who is working together, and on how many tasks?”
  • PT * TT = PT
    • For each person, what tasks are in their critical path?
  • TT * TT = TT
    • Interdependent tasks
  • PT * TT * TP = PP
    • What people are in a person’s critical path?
  • PT * TT * TT’ * TP = PP
    • Who is working together on concurrent tasks?

Structural Balance

  • each edge labeled with either + or −
  • The most plausible

Structural Balance

  • Structural Balance Property
    • For every set of three nodes, if we consider the three edges connecting them, either all three of these edges are labeled +, or else exactly one of them is labeled +
  • Balance Theorem
    • If a labeled complete graph is balanced, then either all pairs of nodes are friends, or else the nodes can be divided into two groups, X and Y , such that every pair of nodes in X like each other, every pair of nodes in Y like each other, and everyone in X is the enemy of everyone in Y
